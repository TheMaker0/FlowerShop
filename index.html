01 — The studio

Grown nearby, gathered early, arranged the same day.

Most flower shops buy from a wholesale cooler that's already days old. We work the opposite way — driving out to three small regional growers before sunrise, choosing stems by hand, and arranging them while the dew is still on the petals.

What this means for you: looser, more honest bouquets that lean and curve the way they actually grew, in colors that shift with the season rather than a catalogue.

It also means we sell out. Often. We'd rather run short on a Tuesday than pad the shelves with flowers flown in from somewhere else.
